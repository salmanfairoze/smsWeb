      <p>
       <b>
        People's Education Society University Ring Road Campus
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        PESU RR
       </b>
       or just
       <b>
        PESU
       </b>
       ), formerly
       <b>
        PES Institute of Technology
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        PESIT
       </b>
       ), is one of three
       <a href="/wiki/Private_university" title="Private university">
        private universities
       </a>
       under the name PES University in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengaluru" title="Bengaluru">
        Bengaluru
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       . Established in 1972,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HOSMAC_Foundation_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-HOSMAC_Foundation-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       it is focused on five main educational areas: Engineering, Medicine, Management, Law and Life Sciences.  The institutions offer both foundation courses in these areas, as well as specializations, with a Bachelors, Master or PhD degree. It was upgraded to a university status in 2013.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=PES_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       PESU RR is managed by the People's Education Society (PES), which founded the institution in 1972, in a rented gymnasium in Bengaluru, with around 40 students. PES currently manages over 45 educational programs in Karnataka and neighboring
       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">
        Andhra Pradesh
       </a>
       , with a total of over 15000 students.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       PESU was founded in the year 1988.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HOSMAC_Foundation_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-HOSMAC_Foundation-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Admission_criteria">
        Admission criteria
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=PES_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Admission criteria">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       PESU admits 40% of its students to undergraduate courses based on their
       <a href="/wiki/Common_Entrance_Test" title="Common Entrance Test">
        Karnataka CET
       </a>
       and 60% seats based on PESSAT (the university's own entrance test) ranks. PESU also admits students under Management quota, which does not place any merit requirements. There is a lateral entry scheme in place, by which students holding diploma degrees can directly enter the second year of study in engineering. On graduating students receive a
       <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">
        Bachelor of Technology
       </a>
       degree.
      </p>
      <p>
       Students are admitted to postgraduate courses based on their
       <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Aptitude_Test_in_Engineering" title="Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering">
        GATE
       </a>
       test scores  as well as on their Post Graduate
       <a href="/wiki/Common_Entrance_Test" title="Common Entrance Test">
        Karnataka CET
       </a>
       scores. Upon graduating, they receive a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_of_Technology" title="Master of Technology">
        Master of Technology
       </a>
       degree.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_and_research">
        Faculty and research
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=PES_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Faculty and research">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Crucible Of Research and Innovation (CORI) is a multi-disciplinary research centre at PESU. It was inaugurated by Bharat Ratna Dr.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.N.R_Rao" title="C.N.R Rao">
        C.N.R Rao
       </a>
       (former Director, Indian Institute of Science) on 7 February 2010. It has research facilities and a set of dedicated research staff who carry out research in their areas of interest.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Centre for Intelligent Systems (CIS) is a research centre at PESU, which researches on topics including Control Systems, Speech, Image and Signal Processing, Robotics, Artificial intelligence, and Low Power VLSI Design.Selected students do internship in Microsoft Innovation Lab in our campus have a great learning curve
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="PISAT">
        PISAT
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=PES_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: PISAT">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       On 26 September 2016, PISAT was successfully placed into polar Sun Synchronous Orbit (SSO) orbit by India's Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle, in its thirty-seventh flight (PSLV-C35). Prime Minister,
       <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">
        Narendra Modi
       </a>
       tweeted "I applaud the industrious and innovative students of PES University, Bengaluru for creating PISAT that was launched successfully today". The launch vehicle, carrying multiple satellites including PISAT, lifted off from
       <a href="/wiki/Sriharikota" title="Sriharikota">
        Sriharikota
       </a>
       in Andhra Pradesh at 9:12 am. The team on the PES University campus is preparing to receive data from the satellite.
      </p>
      <p>
       PISAT is a three axis stabilized imaging
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanosatellite" title="Nanosatellite">
        nanosatellite
       </a>
       weighing about 5 kg and generates 13 watts power with S-band RF communication. The major subsystems are: an imaging camera to provide earth imageries with 80 meter resolution, On Board Computer (OBC), Attitude Determination and Control System (ADCS), RF communication system with S Band frequency, telemetry, telecommand, thermal system, structure and Electrical Power System (EPS). The ground software was developed for on-ground orbit determination essential for mission operations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       PISAT was shipped to
       <a href="/wiki/Satish_Dhawan_Space_Centre" title="Satish Dhawan Space Centre">
        Sriharikota Range
       </a>
       (SHAR) rocket launch centre on 11 September 2016 in the presence of Director of ISRO Satellite Centre (ISAC), Dr. Mylswamy Annadurai, SSS PD Mr. Amareshwar Khened, and other officials. A team of 11 from PES University were present at SHAR for pre-launch activities of PISAT from 11 to 17 September. During the period PISAT was interfaced with an ejection mechanism and integrated with the launch vehicle. Earlier, after stringent performance and quality tests by an expert committee, the nanosatellite was formally handed over to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISRO" title="ISRO">
        ISRO
       </a>
       at a function on the campus attended by top officials from the government and ISRO on 20 April 2016.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Course_content_and_grading">
        Course content and grading
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=PES_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Course content and grading">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       All the courses which have been conferred autonomous status have their departments set their own course content and grading system. The college follows the
       <a href="/wiki/Course_credit" title="Course credit">
        credit-based system
       </a>
       of performance evaluation, with proportional weighting of courses based on their importance. The total marks (usually out of 100) form the basis of grades, with a grade value (out of 10) assigned to a range of marks. For each semester, the students are graded by taking a weighted average of all the subject grades with their respective credit points. Each semester's evaluation is done independently with a cumulative grade point average (CGPA) reflecting the overall performance across semesters. Till June 2012, the grading system being followed was
       <a href="/wiki/Grading_on_a_curve" title="Grading on a curve">
        relative grading
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but the college switched to an absolute grading system after consultations with the university.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
